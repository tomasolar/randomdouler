# Legal Checklist

*One conversation with a local Spanish/Andalusian lawyer covers most of this. Do it early.*

---

## The Core Question

Spanish law distinguishes clearly between:
- A private artist studio hosting cultural gatherings
- A commercial hospitality venue (bar, club, concert space)

Understanding which side of that line you're on — and how to stay there — is the foundation of everything else.

---

## Questions to Clarify with Legal Counsel

### Studio vs. Commercial Venue
- [ ] At what point does a studio gathering legally become a commercial activity?
- [ ] Do recurring events change the classification of the space?
- [ ] Does serving drinks affect the venue classification?
- [ ] Is any form of registration or notification advisable?

### Alcohol
- [ ] Can alcohol be served during cultural events in a studio environment?
- [ ] Does selling drinks require a hospitality license?
- [ ] Can drinks be offered at private events without triggering bar classification?
- [ ] Are there specific limitations for alcohol in non-commercial venues?

### Music & Jam Sessions
- [ ] Are informal jam sessions legally different from live concerts?
- [ ] Do recurring music gatherings require any permit?
- [ ] Does the presence of an audience change the classification?
- [ ] What sound regulations or local ordinances apply?
- [ ] Are there recommended limits on frequency, volume, or audience size?

### Private Events
- [ ] How are private events defined legally in Spain?
- [ ] Are guest lists required?
- [ ] Is alcohol treated differently at private events?
- [ ] Do private cultural gatherings require any form of notification?

### Membership / Private Club Model *(Future)*
- [ ] How are private cultural clubs regulated in Spain?
- [ ] Does a membership structure change how events and hospitality are classified?
- [ ] What legal responsibilities does such a structure involve?

### Noise & Neighbours
- [ ] What are the applicable sound level limits in Marbella?
- [ ] Do music gatherings require notification to the municipality?
- [ ] Are there specific hours or restrictions for studio environments?

---

## The 5 Things That Create Legal Problems (to avoid)

1. **Jam nights that start to look like ticketed concerts** — scheduled bands, stage setups, formal advertising
2. **Alcohol becoming the main event** — bar-style service as the visual centrepiece
3. **Events going fully public** — wide promotion with no distinction between invited guests and the general public
4. **Repeated very large gatherings** — frequency and scale that suggest a regular entertainment venue
5. **Noise complaints from neighbours** — this is usually how legal attention starts

---

## Bottom Line

Operate clearly as a cultural studio with private gatherings and informal jam sessions. That framing, maintained consistently, keeps the space in a defensible position.

When in doubt, keep it private, keep it cultural, and talk to a lawyer before it becomes a problem.
