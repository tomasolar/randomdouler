# Marketing Strategy

*Random Corner does not grow through advertising. It grows through magnetism.*

---

## The Philosophy

The space should never feel like a venue trying to sell events.

It should feel like a living studio environment where art, music, and conversation happen naturally — and occasionally you find out about it through someone you trust.

When people experience something genuine, they talk about it. That's the strategy.

---

## Three Principles

**Authenticity** — Real art, real music, real people. Nothing performed for the camera.

**Cultural Magnetism** — Creative environments attract curious people. Curious people attract more curious people.

**Network Growth** — Every gathering expands the circle. Every visitor is a potential connector.

---

## Who Random Corner Is For

### Creative Locals
Musicians, artists, designers, DJs, creative professionals. These people create the atmosphere. They are the first layer of the ecosystem — get these right and everything else follows.

### Cultural Expats
Marbella has a large Nordic, British, Dutch, and German population. Many are looking for exactly the kind of authentic cultural environment Random Corner offers. They are well-connected and often bridge local and international networks.

### Art Collectors & Art-Curious Visitors
Studio environments are uniquely powerful for this group. They encounter art where it was made, they meet the artist directly, they hear the story. That's what galleries can't offer.

---

## Channels

### Instagram — Primary
Content should show the *life* of the studio, not events to attend.

What works:
- Studio process — paint, materials, work in progress
- Jam night moments — candid, low-fi, real
- Short conversations with visitors or musicians
- New finished works with a sentence about them

What to avoid:
- Promotional flyers
- Anything that looks like a club night
- Overly produced content that feels disconnected from the space

### Word of Mouth — Most Powerful
Happens naturally through jam nights, studio visits, and personal invitations. The goal is to give people something worth talking about.

### Website — Credibility Layer
Simple, clean, always up to date. Concept, artist, artworks, events, contact. The place someone lands when they've heard about Random Corner and want to know if it's real.

---

## The Soft Opening

When the space feels ready — not perfect, ready — host a soft opening for 20–40 people, carefully chosen.

Not for publicity. For connection.

The right 30 people at the right moment is worth more than 200 random attendees.

---

## What to Avoid

- Promoting before the atmosphere exists — it attracts the wrong people
- Going for volume over quality of audience
- Letting the space start to feel commercial or generic

*Strength lies in remaining an authentic cultural environment.*
