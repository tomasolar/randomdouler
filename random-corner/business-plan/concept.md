# The Concept

---

## Three Pillars

Random Corner operates through three interconnected elements that reinforce each other.

### Visual Art

The studio is a working environment first — a place where art is actively being made. Visitors don't enter a curated showroom. They enter a living creative space where finished works hang alongside works in progress, and where the story behind each piece is accessible directly from its maker.

Artwork creation and sales remain the central artistic and economic foundation.

### Music & Cultural Gatherings

Thursday Jam Nights are the social engine of the space. Informal, open, no tickets — musicians from different backgrounds meet and improvise. These evenings are not concerts. They are gatherings.

They attract musicians, artists, curious visitors, and the kinds of people who bring other interesting people with them.

### Social Studio Environment

The atmosphere is the product. Random Corner should feel authentic and welcoming — a place where conversations happen naturally, where collectors encounter art in its original context, and where creative people feel at home rather than observed.

---

## Why Marbella

Marbella sits at an unusual intersection: international wealth, an emerging creative scene, and a near-total absence of independent artist-led cultural spaces.

The city has global visibility but lacks cultural depth. Random Corner exists to fill that gap — not by importing a formula, but by growing something genuine from within it.

---

## The Long Game

> *Growth is intended to remain gradual, authentic, and artist-driven.*

The goal is not to become a venue. The goal is to become a place people talk about — and eventually, a recognized cultural node in Marbella's creative landscape.

Over time this may include artist residencies, institutional collaborations, and connections to European and Nordic cultural funding ecosystems. But those are extensions of something that must first exist and breathe on its own.
