# Revenue Model

*Multiple streams. No single point of failure.*

---

## Fixed Costs (Monthly Baseline)

| Item | Estimate |
|---|---|
| Studio rent | €1,550 |
| Electricity & water | €200–350 |
| Internet, cleaning, supplies | €150–250 |
| Digital & marketing support | €200–400 |
| Content production / documentation | €150–300 |
| **Total baseline** | **≈ €2,300–2,800 / month** |

---

## Revenue Streams

### Artwork Sales
The primary economic driver. Sales occur through studio visits, collector introductions, commissions, and private relationships. The studio environment is an advantage — collectors encounter work directly where it was made, and directly from the artist.

### Thursday Jam Night Hospitality
Beer, wine, simple cocktails. Not a bar — hospitality in support of a cultural gathering. Revenue is modest but consistent once the nights find their rhythm.

Estimated per night: **€300–800** depending on attendance.

### Private Cultural Events
The space can host invitation-based gatherings — art preview evenings, private music nights, corporate creative visits, small celebrations.

Estimated per event: **€3,500–5,000**

## Financial Scenarios

### Scenario 1 — Slow Start *(Pessimistic)*
| Item | Monthly |
|---|---|
| 1 small art sale | €1,200 |
| 4 jam nights @ €300 | €1,200 |
| 1 private event every 2 months | €2,000 avg |
| **Total revenue** | **≈ €4,400** |
| Expenses | ≈ €2,500 |
| **Net** | **≈ €1,900** |

*The project survives. The concept is proven.*

## The Key Variable

None of these scenarios require high volume. They require **cultural gravity** — the kind that builds when people keep returning and bringing others with them.

The snowball effect isn't created through advertising. It emerges when a space consistently offers something real.
