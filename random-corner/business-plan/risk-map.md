# Risk Map

*Knowing where the holes are is the first step to not falling into them.*

---

## Risk 1 — Cash Flow Instability

**What it looks like:** Art sales are irregular. A quiet month with no events creates pressure on rent and operations.

**How to stay ahead:**
- Keep multiple revenue streams active simultaneously
- Never depend solely on art sales
- Even modest jam night hospitality income helps stabilize the baseline

---

## Risk 2 — Growing Too Fast, Too Soon

**What it looks like:** Organizing large events before the atmosphere is established. Spending on upgrades before consistent revenue arrives. Inviting the wrong crowd too early.

**How to stay ahead:**
- Grow through personal invitation, not broad promotion
- Build the cultural core first — the right 15 people beat the wrong 50
- Let the space breathe before scaling it

---

## Risk 3 — Losing the Feel of the Place

**What it looks like:** Events start feeling like a nightclub. Alcohol becomes the main event. The studio starts looking like a generic venue.

**How to stay ahead:**
- Art production stays central — always
- Music and gatherings support the cultural atmosphere, not replace it
- The question to keep asking: *does this still feel like an artist's studio?*

---

## Risk 4 — Legal & Regulatory Missteps

**What it looks like:** Jam nights begin to look like public concerts. Drinks are served without clarity on licensing. Noise complaints trigger inspections.

**How to stay ahead:**
- Consult a local Spanish/Andalusian legal advisor early — one clear conversation prevents most problems
- Operate clearly as: cultural studio → private gatherings → informal jam sessions
- Manage sound levels proactively; most early problems come from neighbors, not inspectors

*See [`legal/checklist.md`](../legal/checklist.md) for specific questions to clarify.*

---

## Risk 5 — Founder Burnout

**What it looks like:** One person carrying too much. No clear split between studio work and operational responsibilities. Financial stress bleeding into creative energy.

**How to stay ahead:**
- Divide roles clearly and honour them (Niko = art direction, Jonathan = music/social)
- Protect deep work time for the studio
- This is a long-term project — it cannot be sprinted into existence

---

## Risk 6 — Network Stops Growing

**What it looks like:** The same 12 people every Thursday. No new faces. The space starts to feel like a closed circle.

**How to stay ahead:**
- Every visitor is a potential connector — make introductions, ask people to bring someone new
- Stay plugged into local artist, musician, and expat communities
- Keep the Instagram active so curious people can find their way in
